# BevAlc Brand Voice Skill

## Overview
This skill defines the writing style, tone, and voice for all BevAlc Intelligence content. Use these guidelines when writing any content that represents the brand.

## Brand Personality

### Core Attributes
1. **Data-Driven** - We lead with numbers, not opinions
2. **Industry Expert** - We know the beverage alcohol business deeply
3. **Accessible** - We make complex data understandable
4. **Objective** - We report, we don't advocate
5. **Efficient** - We respect our readers' time

### Brand Archetype
**The Analyst** - Trusted, knowledgeable, precise, helpful

We're the friend who works in industry data and always knows what's happening before it hits the news.

## Voice Guidelines

### Do
- Use specific numbers and data points
- Explain "what it means" after stating facts
- Reference our database as the source
- Link to relevant SEO pages
- Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences)
- Use industry terminology correctly
- Acknowledge limitations of data

### Don't
- Use marketing hyperbole ("revolutionary", "game-changing")
- Make predictions without data support
- Editorialize about company strategies
- Use excessive jargon without explanation
- Pad content with filler
- Guess at things we don't know
- Use excessive emojis (1-2 max per piece)

## Tone by Content Type

### Weekly Newsletter
**Tone:** Professional but warm, informative but engaging

```
GOOD: "Tequila filings jumped 18% this week, with Diageo
leading the surge. Here's what the data tells us."

BAD: "WOW! Tequila is EXPLODING! Everyone's filing and
it's absolutely incredible to see!"
```

### Company Spotlight
**Tone:** Analytical, thorough, neutral

```
GOOD: "Diageo's Q4 filings reveal a shift toward premium
whiskey extensions, with 23% of new labels in the
'Reserve' or 'Special Edition' naming category."

BAD: "Diageo is crushing it! Their amazing premium strategy
is clearly working and they're dominating the competition."
```

### Trend Report
**Tone:** Educational, data-forward, insightful

```
GOOD: "RTD cocktails have grown from 3% of total filings
in 2020 to 12% in 2025. This five-year shift reflects
changing consumer preferences toward convenience."

BAD: "RTDs are the future! Everyone knows canned cocktails
are where it's at and smart companies are jumping on board."
```

### Absurd Stories
**Tone:** Playful, witty, self-aware

```
GOOD: "The naming committee had been in session for 47 hours.
The coffee was gone. Hope was fading. Then someone
said 'Peacock.'"

BAD: "LOL the name is so random! Like who comes up with
this stuff?? So funny!"
```

## Writing Formulas

### Data Headline Formula
`[Number/Trend] + [Subject] + [Implication/Question]`

Examples:
- "127 New Brands: What's Driving January's Filing Surge?"
- "Tequila Overtakes Vodka in Q4 Filings"
- "3 First-Time Filers to Watch This Week"

### Insight Formula

Example:
"Diageo filed 45 new whiskey labels this quarter (data point),
up 30% from Q3 (context). This aligns with their announced
premium whiskey strategy and may signal new product launches
in the coming months (implication)."

### Trend Formula
`[Trend Statement] + [Supporting Data] + [Industry Context]`

Example:
"RTD cocktails continue their rapid growth (trend).
Filings are up 34% year-over-year, with 892 new labels approved
in Q4 alone (data). This reflects broader consumer demand for
convenience and portability in alcoholic beverages (context)."

## Formatting Standards

### Headlines
- Title case
- 60 characters or less for SEO
- Lead with insight, not just data
- Include category or company when relevant

### Paragraphs
- 2-3 sentences max
- One idea per paragraph
- Use line breaks liberally

### Data Presentation
- Round to meaningful precision (3,245 not 3,245.67)
- Use percentage changes with direction (+12% or -8%)
- Compare to relevant baseline (last week, last quarter, YoY)
- Include absolute numbers for context

### Links
- Link company names to `/company/[slug]`
- Link brand names to `/brand/[slug]`
- Link categories to `/category/[category]/[year]`
- Use descriptive link text, not "click here"

## Vocabulary

### Preferred Terms
| Use | Instead of |
|-----|------------|
| Filings | Applications, submissions |
| Approved | Passed, accepted |
| Category | Segment, type |
| First-time filer | New entrant |
| Filing spike | Surge, explosion |
| Year-over-year | YoY |
| Quarter-over-quarter | QoQ |

### Industry Terms (Use Correctly)
| Term | Correct Usage |
|------|---------------|
| COLA | Certificate of Label Approval (capitalize) |
| TTB | Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau |
| ABV | Alcohol by Volume |
| RTD | Ready-to-drink (cocktails) |
| SKU | Stock Keeping Unit (product variant) |

### Avoid
- "Beverage alcohol space" (just say "industry")
- "Best-in-class" (meaningless)
- "Disruptive" (overused)
- "Synergies" (corporate speak)
- "Innovative" without specifics

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing any content:

- [ ] Contains at least 3 specific data points
- [ ] Data points are sourced from D1/our database
- [ ] No predictions without data support
- [ ] Company/brand names linked to SEO pages
- [ ] Paragraphs are 3 sentences or less
- [ ] Headline is 60 characters or less
- [ ] No marketing hyperbole
- [ ] Limitations acknowledged where relevant
- [ ] Proofread for accuracy
