2025 TTB Filing Year in Review: Bourbon Surges While Overall Launches Decline

January 8, 2026 · 5 min read

We analyzed 140,825 TTB label approvals from 2025. The headline: while the overall beverage alcohol industry saw a 13% decline in filing activity, American whiskey—bourbon in particular—defied the trend with explosive 33% growth in new brand launches.

Here's what the data tells us about where the industry is heading.

32,964
New brands entered the US market in 2025—down 14% from 38,436 in 2024

The Big Picture: A Cooling Market

After years of explosive growth, 2025 marked a clear inflection point. Total TTB COLA filings dropped from 162,472 in 2024 to 140,825 in 2025—a 13% decline.

New brand launches tell an even sharper story: 32,964 new brands in 2025 vs. 38,436 in 2024, representing a 14% pullback. Meanwhile, 1,271 new companies filed their first-ever COLA in 2025, signaling that despite the overall slowdown, new entrants are still betting on the industry.

Metric 2024 2025 Change
Total Filings 162,472 140,825 -13%
New Brands 38,436 32,964 -14%
New Companies — 1,271 —

The Bourbon Boom Continues

Against this backdrop of industry-wide contraction, bourbon is telling a completely different story.

903 new bourbon brands filed for TTB approval in 2025, up from 679 in 2024—a staggering 33% increase. Total bourbon filings (including SKU extensions and refiles) jumped 25% year-over-year, from 2,287 to 2,866.

+33%
Growth in new bourbon brand launches (679 → 903)

What's driving this? A few factors stand out:

Premiumization is accelerating. The data shows a surge in cask-strength, single-barrel, and limited-edition releases—products that command higher margins and attract collector attention.

Regional distilleries are scaling up. We're seeing new filings from states like Wisconsin, Texas, and Nevada—not just Kentucky and Tennessee. The "craft bourbon" narrative is expanding geographically.

Established players are extending lines. Major brands are filing multiple new expressions, suggesting confidence in consumer demand for variety within the bourbon category.

Notable New Bourbon Entrants (December 2025)

  • Assemblage Bourbon (Texas) — Launched cask-strength and standard expressions
  • RNOW Rare (Wisconsin) — New craft entrant
  • Blackhat Distillery — Barrel-strength and standard bourbon
  • Snake Creek Single Barrel — Small-batch positioning

Tequila: Steady Growth Continues

Tequila maintained its momentum, though at a more measured pace than bourbon. 491 new tequila brands entered the market in 2025, up 11% from 444 in 2024.

Total tequila filings rose from 1,945 to 2,125 (+9%), and 60 new companies filed their first tequila COLA this year—indicating that despite the crowded market, new entrants still see opportunity.

What This Means for 2026

The data suggests a market in transition. The post-pandemic "spirits gold rush" is cooling, but capital is concentrating in categories with strong consumer pull—particularly American whiskey.

For suppliers and distributors: The bourbon pipeline is full. Expect fierce competition for shelf space as these 900+ new brands seek distribution in 2026.

For brands: The overall decline in filing activity suggests consolidation ahead. Companies with weak distribution or undifferentiated positioning will struggle as retailer patience wanes.

For investors: The 1,271 new company filings represent real money entering the market. But with overall activity down, due diligence is more important than ever.

Methodology

This analysis is based on TTB COLA (Certificate of Label Approval) filings from January 1 through December 31 of each year. "New brands" are defined as brand names that appear in our database for the first time from a given company. "New companies" are entities filing their first-ever COLA.
